The Process

1. IDEA - When a new project (course) idea is available, typically a SME, AD, or Program Head from the School and an IDC will meet to discuss the project and its deliverables

School operational plan - workload. IDC assigned. Help with course design. Including content and activities.

Other resources include graphic designers, editors, 3D, interactive, video, etc.

The Process

2. LTC PROJECT - LAT team is notified, the project is entered into our project management system, and is on our radar for future development.

The Process

3. DESIGN & DEVELOP - Content is worked on (designed, written, edited, multimedia all built, etc)

The Process

4. CONTENT IS READY - content is passed off to LAT team. We estimate the time needed to complete the tasks, then schedule the task(s) into our internal production queue and assign it to a LAS to work on.

The Process

5. PRODUCTION - Learning Application Specialist builds out course for online delivery.

The Process

6. Q&A - If course requires revisions, a new task can be created and assigned to a LAS for completion.

The Process

7. COURSE COMPLETED - course is fully built out and handed off to SME. Discussions surrounding support and maintenance.

Moving Forward

Content is King!

Production-ready content

Content is the information students are seeking which mainly consists of text, media and interactivities, we suggest not focusing on visual styling of the course, such as banners, colours, fonts, icons, layout as that's secondary to content - we have also provided a institute branded styling for your course as default. This is one less thing for you to think about so you can focus on content.

Production-ready content is content that's been reviewed, proofread, quality-tested and follows Word formatting recommended in this user-guide. This will guarantee a quicker turnaround and reduce revisions and interations. Ultimately, this content will be published for your students to consume on D2L. Like Imagine your content getting ready for the printers. You want your audience to see a finished and refined product

Follow the Word examples in the Conversion Guide when creating your content in Word.
